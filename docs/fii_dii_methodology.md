# FII/DII Flow Methodology

When interpreting today's FII/DII print — open this file first. Do not characterise flow regimes from training-time knowledge.

---

## 1. Participant Profiles

### FII (~17-20% of NSE market cap)

**Who they are:**
- SEBI Cat I (sovereign funds, central banks), Cat II (broad-based funds, endowments), Cat III (hedge funds, proprietary)
- Aggregate AUM running through India exposure: ~$600-700bn at peak
- Concentration: Financials (HDFC, ICICI, Kotak, Axis) ~30% of FII equity, IT (Infy, TCS, Wipro, HCL) ~20%, Reliance ~5-7%, large consumer names

**What moves them:**
- US Federal Reserve rate trajectory (DXY correlation)
- India relative valuation vs other EMs (Nifty PE vs MSCI EM PE)
- India macro: CAD, fiscal deficit, GDP growth, INR stability
- MSCI/FTSE index rebalances (quarterly: Feb/May/Aug/Nov)
- Seasonal patterns: March FY-end redemptions, December year-end outflows, January fresh allocations

**FII selling triggers:**
- US Fed hike or hawkish pivot → DXY rises → EM outflows
- DXY >105-107 (sustained)
- Global risk-off: VIX spike, credit spread widening
- India tax/regulatory surprise (retrospective tax, SEBI new category limits)
- Nifty trailing PE >22-23× (rich vs EM peers)
- Better EM opportunity opens up (China reopening rotation — Oct 2024)

**FII herding behavior:** When one large FII (Vanguard, Blackrock, GIC Singapore) announces India underweight, others follow within 1-2 weeks. The daily print alone won't tell you this — look at 5-day rolling trend.

---

### DII (~15-17% of market cap)

**Who they are:**
- Mutual Funds: ~60%+ of DII AUM, total AUM >₹60L cr. SBI MF, ICICI Prudential, HDFC MF are the big three
- LIC: single largest DII participant, ~₹40L cr+ equity book
- Private insurers: Bajaj Allianz, HDFC Life, Max Life — multi-decade horizons
- EPFO, NPS, pension corpus: ~₹8-10L cr+ combined

**DII behavior:**
- Counter-cyclical by design: MFs buy on dips (SIP deploying), sell on rallies (profit-booking/rebalancing)
- Less sensitive to global macro — driven by domestic AUM inflow
- EPFO/NPS are pension money — almost never forced sellers
- LIC is a special case: can hold indefinitely, used as a government instrument in extreme selloffs

## 2. DII Absorption Capacity — The SIP Floor Math

This is the rule that lets you interpret a single day's FII print in context.

**Monthly steady-state DII demand:**

| Source | Monthly inflow |
|--------|----------------|
| SIP (equity schemes) | ~₹25,000-26,000 cr |
| Insurance (new premium + renewal) | ~₹5,000-10,000 cr |
| EPFO (85% equity allocation) | ~₹3,000-5,000 cr |
| Lump-sum MF + NFOs | variable (₹5,000-25,000 cr) |
| **Total structural floor** | **~₹35,000-40,000 cr/month** |

**Daily conversion:** ~₹1,500-2,000 cr/trading day (structural floor, regardless of market level).

**Absorption tier logic:**

| FII daily selling | DII response | Net outcome |
|------------------|--------------|-------------|
| <₹1,500 cr | Fully absorbed by structural SIP flow | Market holds flat or recovers |
| ₹1,500-4,000 cr | Partial absorption; some residual selling | Moderate pressure, -0.3 to -0.8% intraday |
| ₹4,000-7,000 cr | Absorption overwhelmed | Market -0.8 to -2% |
| >₹7,000 cr | DII buying itself slows (fear + redemptions) | Sharp fall -2-5%; can cascade |

**Oct 2024 caveat:** Even 100%+ DII absorption (₹96,200 cr vs ₹94,000 cr FII selling) produced -6.2% on Nifty. Above ~₹80-90K cr/month FII selling, retail panic amplifies beyond what net flows predict. The threshold is not just the institutional math — it's retail behavior at that level.

## 3. The 6-Regime Classifier

Assign a regime label to the current FII/DII picture before interpreting any alert.

### Regime 1: FII Net Buyer (35-40% of months historically)

**Criteria (any of):**
- FII net positive for 5+ consecutive trading days
- MTD FII net > +₹5,000 cr
- 20-day rolling sum of FII flows: positive AND rising slope

**Market characteristics:**
- Nifty uptrend or upward-biased consolidation
- INR strengthens (84→82 range)
- Large-caps outperform (Nifty 50 > Nifty Midcap 150)
- Bank Nifty leads (highest FII weight)
- High-FII-ownership names benefit most

---

### Regime 2: FII Net Seller (30-35% of months)

**Criteria (any of):**
- FII net negative for 5+ consecutive trading days
- MTD FII net < -₹5,000 cr
- 20-day rolling sum: negative AND falling slope

**Market characteristics:**
- Nifty under pressure; DII buying limits but does not eliminate downside
- INR weakens
- Large-caps underperform vs mid/small (less FII exposure below Nifty 100)
- IT names sometimes outperform (INR depreciation = USD revenue translation)
- If daily selling >₹3,000 cr sustained: market -1-3%/week

**Portfolio read:** Low-FII-ownership names (e.g. defence) have a structural cushion → relative outperformer in this regime.

---

### Regime 3: DII Absorption (15-20% of months)

**Criteria (all of):**
- FII net negative for 5+ trading days
- DII net positive (counter-buying)
- Absorption ratio >70% (DII buying / FII selling)
- Nifty: stable or range-bound (not trending down sharply)

**Market characteristics:**
- Choppy sideways range; neither bulls nor bears win decisively
- Sector rotation: DII-favored sectors (FMCG, Pharma, PSU banks) outperform
- Resolves when: FII selling exhausts (regime flips to Buyer) OR DII slows (regime flips to Seller)
- Duration: 7-21 trading days typically

---

### Regime 4: Dual Buying (10-15% of months) — most bullish, shortest-lived

**Criteria:**
- Both FII AND DII net positive for 3+ consecutive trading days
- Combined net > +₹3,000 cr/day

**Market characteristics:**
- Strong sustained rally: +1-3% per week
- Broad-based participation: mid-caps participate alongside large-caps
- A/D ratio >2:1; India VIX declining
- Duration: typically 7-15 trading days before one side slows

**Portfolio read:** ALL positions benefit; Core adds justified here if fundamentals support. The rarest and most powerful regime.

---

### Regime 5: Dual Selling (<5% of months) — crisis mode

**Criteria:**
- Both FII AND DII net negative for 3+ consecutive trading days
- Combined net < -₹2,000 cr/day

**Market characteristics:**
- Sharp correction: -3-7%/week
- India VIX spikes >25
- Liquidity dries up; bid-ask spreads widen
- Historical examples: COVID March 2020, Lehman spillover (2008), major domestic fraud revelations

**Portfolio read:** Cash is king. No new buys until DII re-enters. Only Core % survives on conviction.

---

### Regime 6: Transition Phase (10-15% of months)

**Criteria:**
- FII direction switching within last 5 trading days (was net selling, now buying, or vice versa)
- Alternating positive/negative days with no trend
- Daily absolute values shrinking (₹500-1,500 cr range)

**Market characteristics:**
- Market at inflection — need confirmation before acting
- **Do not call a new regime until 3+ consecutive days in new direction**

## 4. Daily/MTD/YTD Significance Thresholds

Use these to calibrate whether a day's print is signal or noise.

### Daily FII net (₹ crore)

| Range | Category | Market impact |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| ±0-500 | Noise | Negligible |
| ±500-1,000 | Mild | Minor influence |
| ±1,000-2,000 | Moderate | Contributes to daily direction |
| ±2,000-5,000 | Significant | Strong directional push |
| Beyond ±5,000 | Extreme | Market moves 1%+; regime-defining |

### Monthly MTD (₹ crore)

| Range | Trend classification |
|-------|---------------------|
| ±0-5,000 | Balanced month |
| ±5,000-10,000 | Moderate trend forming |
| ±10,000-25,000 | Strong trend |
| Beyond ±25,000 | Major trend — regime analysis required |

### Yearly YTD (₹ crore)

| Range | Strategic classification |
|-------|--------------------------|
| ±0-25,000 | Neutral year |
| ±25,000-50,000 | Moderate FII positioning |
| ±50,000-1,00,000 | Significant structural shift |
| Beyond ±1,00,000 | Structural — check MSCI weight changes |

**Regime adjustment:** In high-VIX environments (India VIX >20), apply ±50% to all thresholds — flows have larger price impact when liquidity is thin.

## 5. Four Historical Case Studies — The Patterns That Repeat

### COVID March 2020

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| FII selling | ~₹65,000 cr in one month; peak days >₹7,000 cr |
| DII buying | ~₹55,000 cr (absorption ratio ~85%) |
| Nifty impact | 12,000 → 7,500 (-37.5%) |
| USD/INR | 72 → 76 |
| India VIX | 15 → 85 |

**Lesson:** Even strong DII counter-buying cannot prevent crash when FII panic exceeds absorption capacity AND retail follows. Recovery was swift once FII returned — FY2021 saw ₹2.74L cr net FII inflow. March 2020 low was a generational buying opportunity.

---

### FY2022 (Ukraine + Fed Hike Cycle)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| FII selling | ~₹1.4L cr over 10 months (drawn out, not panic) |
| DII buying | ~₹2.7L cr — **first time DII decisively overpowered FII selling** |
| Nifty impact | -15% peak to trough; recovered within 4 months |
| USD/INR | 74 → 82 |
| Mid/small-cap performance | Outperformed large-caps significantly |

**Lesson:** India's structural FII-dependence has declined as DII AUM has scaled. For the first time, DII out-bought FII selling by nearly 2:1 on an annual basis. INR however remained FII-sensitive (falling despite DII equity support). Portfolio implication: mid-caps with low FII ownership (DEFENSE names, smaller IT) held up better.

---

### October 2024 (China Stimulus Rotation)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| FII selling | ~₹94,000 cr in one month (single-month record) |
| DII buying | ~₹96,200 cr (absorption ratio >100%) |
| Nifty impact | **-6.2% despite full DII absorption** |
| USD/INR | Breached 84 |

**Lesson:** There is a threshold (~₹80-90K cr/month FII selling) above which full institutional absorption STILL cannot prevent decline — retail panic amplifies the move regardless of net flows. This was also China-specific: FII rotated to China believing stimulus was transformative. Portfolio implication: names with high FII ownership were hit disproportionately.

---

### FY2021 (Post-COVID FII Return)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| FII inflow | +₹2.74L cr (record annual inflow at the time) |
| Monthly flows | ₹15,000-25,000 cr consistently positive |
| Nifty performance | 8,600 → 15,000 (+75%) |
| INR | 76 → 73 (strengthened) |
| IPO market | Boom; SME listings at 50-100× oversubscription |

**Lesson:** When FII flows align with improving fundamentals + global liquidity flush, India rally is powerful and broad-based. FY2021 created multi-baggers across every sector. The signal at the bottom (FY2020 end) was DII Absorption regime + VIX >35 + promoter buybacks.
